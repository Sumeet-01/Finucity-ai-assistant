Section 80C allows deductions up to ₹1,50,000 per year for: EPF/VPF contributions, PPF deposits (up to ₹1.5L/year), ELSS mutual funds (3-year lock-in), NSC (5-year post office scheme), 5-year Fixed Deposits, Life insurance premiums, Sukanya Samriddhi (for girl child), Home loan principal repayment, tuition fees for up to 2 children. Additionally, Section 80CCD(1B) allows an extra ₹50,000 deduction for NPS contributions — beyond the 80C limit. So your total deduction potential under 80C + 80CCD(1B) is ₹2,00,000.

HRA exemption is the minimum of three values: (1) Actual HRA received from employer, (2) 50% of basic salary if you live in a metro city (Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata) or 40% for non-metro, (3) Actual rent paid minus 10% of basic salary. For example: if your basic is ₹50,000/month, HRA is ₹20,000, and rent is ₹25,000 in Delhi — your HRA exemption would be min(₹20,000, ₹25,000, ₹20,000) = ₹20,000/month. SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a method of investing a fixed amount every month in mutual funds. The power lies in compounding — ₹10,000/month at 12% for 20 years creates ₹1 Crore (invested only ₹24L). Our SIP Calculator takes your monthly investment amount, expected annual return, and duration to project the total corpus. It also shows the wealth gained (corpus minus investment) so you can visualize the compounding effect. Advanced features include step-up SIP (increasing investment by a percentage annually) and inflation adjustment.
