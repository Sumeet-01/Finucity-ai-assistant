GST Framework at a Glance

India's Unified Indirect Tax System

GST replaced 17 indirect taxes (Service Tax, VAT, Excise, CST, etc.) with a single, unified tax structure. For businesses, this means one registration, one return framework, and seamless Input Tax Credit across the supply chain. Compliance is critical — late filing attracts ₹200/day late fees and 18% interest on unpaid tax.

📋 Registration & Setup

Mandatory for businesses with turnover above ₹40L (goods) or ₹20L (services). We handle end-to-end registration, HSN/SAC code mapping, and invoice format setup.

✓ New GST registration (regular/composition)
✓ Multi-state GSTIN for inter-state business
✓ Voluntary registration for startups (to claim ITC)
✓ Amendment, cancellation, and revocation services
Startup Tip: Even if below threshold, register voluntarily to claim Input Tax Credit on expenses and appear more professional to B2B clients.

📅 Return Filing & Compliance

GST requires monthly/quarterly returns with precise data matching. GSTR-1 (outward supplies), GSTR-3B (summary + payment), and GSTR-9 (annual) — all with strict deadlines.

✓ GSTR-1: Monthly by 11th (outward supplies)
✓ GSTR-3B: Monthly by 20th (summary return + tax payment)
✓ GSTR-9/9C: Annual return + reconciliation (31st Dec)
✓ QRMP Scheme: Quarterly returns for turnover < ₹5 Cr
Penalty Alert: Late GSTR-3B = ₹50/day (₹20 for nil) + 18% interest. GSTR-1 delay can block your buyer's ITC claims.

💰 Input Tax Credit Optimization

ITC is the backbone of GST — pay tax only on the value you add. But ITC claims are frequently rejected due to mismatches. We reconcile GSTR-2B with your purchase data to maximize legitimate claims.

✓ GSTR-2B reconciliation (auto-populated ITC ledger)
✓ Vendor compliance monitoring (ensure suppliers file)
✓ Blocked credit identification (Section 17(5))
✓ ITC reversal rules (Rule 42/43 for mixed supplies)
Key Rule: ITC is available only if your supplier has filed their GSTR-1 AND paid their tax. Monitor vendor compliance monthly.

📄 E-Invoicing & E-Way Bills

E-invoicing is mandatory for businesses with turnover above ₹5 Cr. Every B2B invoice must be registered on the IRP portal and get an IRN (Invoice Reference Number). E-way bills are required for goods movement above ₹50,000.

✓ E-invoice generation via IRP/NIC portal
✓ IRN & QR code integration with billing software
✓ E-way bill generation for goods transport
✓ Multi-vehicle & consolidated e-way bills
2024 Update: E-invoicing threshold reduced to ₹5 Cr. If your turnover crossed this in any FY from 2017-18, e-invoicing is mandatory.

Schemes

Composition vs Regular Scheme

Composition Scheme

  • Turnover up to ₹1.5 Cr (goods) / ₹50L (services)
  • Pay flat 1-6% tax on turnover
  • Quarterly returns only (CMP-08)
  • Cannot claim Input Tax Credit
  • Cannot do inter-state supplies
  • No e-invoicing required

Regular Scheme

  • No turnover limit
  • Pay tax at applicable slab rate
  • Monthly returns (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B)
  • Can claim full Input Tax Credit
  • Can do inter-state & export supplies
  • E-invoicing mandatory if turnover > ₹5Cr
