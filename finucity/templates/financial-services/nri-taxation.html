Understanding NRI Tax Residency

Who is an NRI for Tax Purposes?

Under Section 6 of the Income Tax Act, your residential status — not citizenship — determines your tax liability. If you've spent less than 182 days in India during the financial year (or less than 60 days if you left India for employment), you're considered a Non-Resident.

✓Resident (ROR)

182+ days in India. Taxed on worldwide income.

⚠RNOR

Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident. Taxed on India income + income from business controlled in India.

✈Non-Resident (NRI)

Less than 182 days. Taxed only on Indian income — salary received in India, rental income, capital gains on Indian assets.

NRI Tax Service Areas

🏠 Property Sale & Capital Gains

Selling property in India? Buyer deducts 20% TDS (+ surcharge & cess) on long-term capital gains. Proper computation, indexation benefits, and Section 54/54EC reinvestment exemptions can significantly reduce your tax outgo.

✓ LTCG indexation benefit (Cost Inflation Index adjustment)
✓ Section 54: Reinvest in new house within 2 years
✓ Section 54EC: Invest in NHAI/REC bonds (₹50L cap)
✓ Lower TDS certificate (Form 13) to reduce withholding
Common Issue: Buyers often deduct TDS on full sale value instead of capital gains. We help file for correct refund.

🌐 DTAA & Double Taxation Relief

India has Double Taxation Avoidance Agreements with 90+ countries. These prevent you from being taxed twice on the same income. Proper treaty application can reduce your effective tax rate significantly.

✓ US: India-US DTAA (Article 10 Dividends, Article 12 Royalties)
✓ UK: India-UK DTAA (Capital Gains, Employment Income)
✓ UAE/Singapore/Canada: Specific treaty benefits for each
✓ Tax Credit: Claim foreign tax paid against Indian tax liability
Key Requirement: You need a Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) from your country of residence to claim DTAA benefits.

💰 Rental Income & TDS

NRI rental income is taxed at slab rates (not flat 30%). Tenants/property managers must deduct 30% TDS under Section 195 before paying rent to NRIs. We help optimize and claim refunds.

✓ 30% standard deduction on rental income
✓ Home loan interest deduction (Section 24b)
✓ Municipal tax deduction
✓ TDS refund if actual tax < TDS deducted
Pro Tip: If your total Indian income is below ₹10L, effective tax is much less than 30%. File ITR to claim the substantial refund.

💳 FEMA, Repatriation & NRO/NRE

Money movement between India and abroad is governed by FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act). The rules around NRO vs NRE accounts, repatriation limits, and LRS are critical for compliance.

✓ NRE Account: Freely repatriable, tax-free interest
✓ NRO Account: ₹1 Cr/year repatriation limit, interest taxable
✓ Form 15CA/15CB: Mandatory for foreign remittances
✓ LRS: ,000/year liberalized remittance scheme
Important: Repatriation of property sale proceeds requires CA certificate (Form 15CB) and RBI compliance.

NRI Tax Filing Checklist

01

Determine Residential Status

Count days in India. Below 182 (or 60 for seafarers/employment abroad) = NRI status for that FY.

02

Identify Indian Income Sources

Rental income, capital gains, FD interest (NRO), dividend income, business income from India operations.

03

Collect TDS Certificates

Form 16A from tenants, banks (NRO interest), equity brokers, property buyers. Cross-check with Form 26AS/AIS.

04

Apply DTAA Benefits

Get Tax Residency Certificate from country of residence. Apply lower treaty rates where applicable.

05

File ITR & Claim Refunds

Use ITR-2 (most NRIs). Due date: July 31. Claim refund if TDS deducted exceeds actual tax liability.

06

Repatriation Compliance

File Form 15CA/15CB for remittances. Ensure NRO-to-NRE transfers comply with RBI guidelines (₹1Cr/year).
