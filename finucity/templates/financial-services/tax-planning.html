Our Approach

Holistic Tax Management

Tax planning is not merely about saving tax under Section 80C. It is a year-round, strategic process of aligning every rupee you earn, invest, and spend with tax-efficiency. With the New Tax Regime (Section 115BAC) becoming the default from FY 2023-24 onwards, the paradigm has shifted from "forced saving via deductions" to "conscious investing with lower rates."

Our approach evaluates your complete financial profile — salary structure, capital gains, rental income, business receipts, and existing investments — to mathematically determine whether the Old Regime (with deductions like 80C, 80D, 24b, HRA) or the New Regime (with reduced slab rates and ₹75,000 standard deduction) leaves more cash in your hand. The objective is not to blindly invest in tax-saving instruments but to invest smartly where your money grows while also saving tax.

Regime Selection Analysis

Standard Deduction is now ₹75,000 under New Regime. We run a side-by-side mathematical comparison factoring in your HRA, 80C, 80D, NPS, and home loan deductions to determine your optimal regime. The breakeven point is typically around ₹3.75 Lakh in total deductions.

Capital Gains Harvesting

Strategically book Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) from equity each year to utilize the ₹1.25 Lakh annual tax-free exemption. This technique resets your cost base, effectively making future gains tax-free — a wealth compounding multiplier used by sophisticated investors.

🏥

Health & Insurance Optimization

Maximize Section 80D deductions up to ₹1 Lakh by covering self (₹25K), family (₹25K), and senior citizen parents (₹50K) with adequate health insurance. Add preventive health check-up claims of ₹5,000 within the overall limit for complete coverage.

Old Regime

Core Tax Saving Instruments

Instruments available under the Old Tax Regime for systematic reduction of taxable income

Section 80C

Max: ₹1,50,000
ELSS Mutual Funds

Lowest lock-in period (3 years) among all 80C instruments with potential equity returns of 12-15% CAGR. Tax-saving with wealth creation — the gold standard for aggressive savers.

PPF (Public Provident Fund)

Government-backed, sovereign guarantee with EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) tax status. 15-year lock-in with partial withdrawal from year 7. Current rate: 7.1% compounded annually.

EPF (Employee Provident Fund)

Mandatory employer-employee contribution. Your 12% contribution counts under 80C. Employer match is additional benefit. Interest rate ~8.15% — one of the best risk-free returns available.

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana

For girl child (below 10 years). Currently highest fixed-income rate among small savings schemes (~8.2%). 21-year maturity with partial withdrawal for education at age 18. Triple tax benefit (EEE).

Home Loan Principal Repayment

EMI principal component qualifies under 80C. Combined with Sec 24(b) interest deduction of ₹2L, a home loan becomes one of the most powerful tax-saving instruments for homebuyers.

Life Insurance Premiums / Term Plans

Premiums paid on term insurance for self, spouse, and children qualify. Ensure you buy pure term insurance (not ULIPs or endowment plans) — low cost, high cover is the mantra.

Beyond Section 80C

Additional Savings
Sec 80CCD(1B) — NPS

Additional ₹50,000 deduction exclusively for National Pension System (NPS) Tier-1 contributions. This is over and above the ₹1.5L limit of 80C. Effective total deduction: ₹2,00,000 across 80C + 80CCD(1B).

Sec 80D — Medical Insurance

Self/Family: ₹25,000 | Senior Citizen Parents: ₹50,000 | Health Check-up: ₹5,000 (within overall limit). Maximum combined deduction: ₹1,00,000. Covers Mediclaim, Critical Illness, and Top-up policies.

Sec 24(b) — Home Loan Interest

Deduction up to ₹2,00,000 on interest paid for self-occupied property. For let-out property, the entire interest amount is deductible without limit (loss from house property capped at ₹2L for set-off against other income).

Sec 80E — Education Loan Interest

No upper limit on deduction. Available for 8 years from the year of first repayment. Covers higher education loans for self, spouse, children, or a student for whom you are the legal guardian.

Sec 80G — Charitable Donations

50% or 100% deduction for donations to approved institutions. PM CARES Fund, National Defence Fund qualify for 100% deduction. Ensure you get an 80G receipt with the institution's PAN and approval number.

Sec 80TTA/80TTB — Savings Interest

80TTA: ₹10,000 deduction on savings account interest for individuals. 80TTB: ₹50,000 for senior citizens on all bank deposit interest (FD, RD, savings). Highly underutilized deductions.

For Salaried Employees

Salary Structure Optimization

Restructuring CTC components to maximize take-home pay under the Old Regime

🏠

HRA Exemption Planning

One of the largest single tax-saving components for salaried individuals living in rented accommodation. The exemption is the minimum of three values:

  • Actual HRA received from employer
  • 50% of Basic Salary (Metro) or 40% (Non-metro)
  • Rent Paid minus 10% of Basic Salary
Pro Tip: You can legally pay rent to your parents (if they own the house) and claim HRA. They must declare it as rental income, but the net family tax saving is often significant.
🍴

Meal Vouchers & Food Coupons

Meal vouchers (Sodexo/Ticket Restaurant) provided by your employer are exempt up to ₹50 per meal. For 22 working days per month, this translates to ₹26,400 annually tax-free — a completely legal and often overlooked benefit.

Pro Tip: Request your HR to include meal coupons in your CTC restructuring during appraisal season. It directly reduces taxable income.
🚗

Company Car Lease Policy

Opting for a company-leased car saves tax on EMI, maintenance, insurance, and even driver salary — all paid from pre-tax CTC components. The perquisite value charged is significantly lower than the actual benefit received.

Pro Tip: Compare the total tax saving against taking a car loan yourself. For vehicles above ₹10L, the lease route almost always wins.
✈

LTA (Leave Travel Allowance)

Exemption can be claimed twice in a block of 4 years for domestic travel (travel fare only, not hotel/food). Current block: 2022-2025. Covers air, rail, or road fare for self and immediate family.

Pro Tip: If you didn't claim in the current block, you can carry one unclaimed journey to the first year of the next block.
